The short answer A pervasive sense of impending doom without clear cause is usually less about prediction and more about a nervous system that's learned to stay braced for threat. It can also be a form of anticipatory grief — protecting yourself from the shock of bad news by imagining it first.

Frequently asked

Why do I feel like something bad is about to happen?

This can be anxiety presenting as premonition — the nervous system generates a sense of threat without a specific object. It can also be a learned response: if your environment was unpredictable in the past, 'waiting for it' became adaptive. In the present, that waiting continues even when the environment is safer.

Is this feeling a bad sign or just anxiety?

Often just anxiety, especially if it's persistent and unattached to anything specific. Genuine intuition tends to be quiet and specific; anxiety tends to be loud, vague, and constant. The feeling of something bad coming is a common anxiety symptom rather than reliable prediction.

How do I stop feeling this way?

Not by reassuring yourself it won't happen — that only works briefly. More useful is noticing the feeling, naming it ('that's the dread again'), and grounding in what's actually present right now. Over time, this trains the nervous system that the alarm isn't always accurate.
