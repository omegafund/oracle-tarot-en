Why Do I Feel Like Nothing Will Ever Change?

Created as a space for personal reflection — not therapy or diagnosis.

You've been here before. Many times.

And it always feels like this is just how it is.

The hope that things might be different has gotten very quiet.

The short answer The feeling that nothing will ever change is one of the signature signs of depression — but it's also something many people feel in difficult periods. It's almost never accurate as a prediction.
Name one thing — even small — that has changed in the last year. What does that tell you?
🔄 Updated July 2026

What this reflection can reveal

  • the difference between how things feel right now and how they're likely to unfold
  • whether depression or depletion might be a factor
  • what small evidence of change might already exist that the current feeling is filtering out

Signs this may be true for you

  • You feel like your situation is fixed, even when you can acknowledge it's changed before.
  • Good news feels temporary; bad news feels permanent.
  • The feeling is worse when you're tired, isolated, or overwhelmed.

Frequently asked

Why do I feel like things will never get better?

This feeling is often a symptom of emotional depletion or depression rather than an accurate prediction.

Is this feeling a sign of depression?

It can be — the belief that things won't change is one of depression's central distortions.

How do I find hope when I can't feel it?

Usually not through positive thinking. What helps more is small evidence: identifying one thing that has changed.
