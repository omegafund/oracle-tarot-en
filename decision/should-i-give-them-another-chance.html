Frequently asked

How do I know if a second chance is a good idea?

Look at what specifically has changed, not what's been promised. Change is behavioral and consistent over time, not a declaration in a moment of desperation. If the answer to 'what's different' is mostly emotional intensity or promises, that's important information.

Is it weak to give someone a second chance?

No — giving a second chance from a genuine belief that something has shifted is a form of wisdom and generosity. The question is whether you're giving it from that place or from a place of avoiding the pain of ending it. Those feel different from the inside if you're honest.

How many chances is too many?

When you're asking this question seriously, you often already know something. The number isn't the point; the pattern is. If the cycle of harm, apology, and return is well-established and the repair phase is consistently followed by the same behavior, that's the information.
