Why Do I Feel Guilty for Wanting More?

Created as a space for personal reflection — not therapy or diagnosis.

You want something different from what you have.

A better relationship. A bigger life.

And somewhere alongside the wanting is guilt.

The short answer Guilt for wanting more is usually about a belief that your current situation is 'enough' and wanting more is a failure of gratitude — or a fear that wanting more criticizes those who gave you what you have.
What specifically would wanting more require you to change — and is any of the guilt about what that change would mean for others?
🔄 Updated July 2026

What this reflection can reveal

  • where the 'wanting is ungrateful' belief came from
  • the difference between gratitude and settling
  • what you actually want, underneath the guilt

Signs this may be true for you

  • You feel guilty when you imagine a life different from the one you have.
  • You minimize your wants when talking to others.
  • You feel most guilty about wanting more in the areas that matter most to you.

Frequently asked

Is wanting more selfish?

No — wanting more is how people grow and build lives that fit who they actually are.

Why do I feel guilty for wanting things?

Often because wanting was once unsafe or unwelcome — in an environment where you were taught to be grateful for what you had.

How do I stop feeling guilty for wanting more?

By separating the wanting from the action. You're allowed to want things.
