Why Do I Always Overthink Before Making a Decision?

Created as a space for personal reflection — not therapy or diagnosis.

You think it through. Then again. Then you consider angles you hadn't thought of.

The more you think, the less clear it gets.

And the decision that should have taken an hour has taken a week.

The short answer Overthinking before decisions is usually less about the decision and more about intolerance of uncertainty — the belief that enough thinking will eliminate the risk that any choice carries.

Frequently asked

Why do I overthink decisions?

Usually because the problem isn't the decision — it's the uncertainty that any decision leaves. Overthinking is an attempt to eliminate that uncertainty through more analysis.

Does overthinking lead to better decisions?

Research suggests it often doesn't — beyond a certain threshold of deliberation, more thinking introduces noise rather than clarity.

How do I make decisions without overthinking?

By setting a deliberation limit: give yourself the information you can reasonably gather, a defined time period, and then make the best call you can with what you have.
