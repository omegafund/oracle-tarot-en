Why Do I Keep Making the Same Mistakes?

Created as a space for personal reflection — not therapy or diagnosis.

You swore you wouldn't do it again. And then you did.

Not out of stupidity. Not because you forgot. For a reason you couldn't quite see in the moment.

That reason is worth finding.

The short answer Repeating mistakes isn't evidence of stupidity or weakness — it's usually evidence that the 'mistake' is meeting a need, resolving a feeling, or following a script that was written before you consciously chose it.
What does the mistake give you in the moment — before the consequences arrive?

Signs this may be true for you

  • The same situation keeps appearing in different forms.
  • You can see the pattern clearly from the outside, but not from inside it.
  • You feel genuinely surprised when it happens again, even though it's happened before.
  • You know what you 'should' do but find yourself doing something else.

Frequently asked

Why do I repeat the same mistakes?

Because what looks like a mistake from the outside often looks like a solution from the inside. The behavior — whether it's going back to someone, avoiding a hard conversation, or reaching for comfort in a damaging way — is doing something for you in the moment. Understanding what that is is the beginning of changing it.

Is repeating mistakes a sign of low intelligence?

No — it's often a sign of unresolved emotional patterns or unmet needs. Many highly intelligent people repeat relationship patterns or self-defeating behaviors because intelligence isn't what addresses those roots.

How do I stop repeating the same pattern?

The first shift is noticing it while it's happening rather than only after. That moment of recognition — 'here it is again' — is where choice becomes possible. Then: what would a different response look like, and what would it cost you in the short term? The short-term cost is usually why the pattern persists.
