Why Do I Always Listen to Everyone Else Instead of Myself?

Created as a space for personal reflection — not therapy or diagnosis.

You ask. Then you ask someone else. Then someone else.

At the end of all the asking, you're not clearer — you're more confused.

You're looking for someone to give you permission to do what you already know you want.

The short answer Constantly seeking others' opinions is usually a sign that your own judgment was undermined at some point — told it was wrong, or met with consequences when it was followed.
What would you decide if you weren't allowed to ask anyone else?
🔄 Updated July 2026

What this reflection can reveal

  • where self-trust was undermined
  • what you already know that you're trying to get permission for
  • the difference between gathering information and avoiding your own knowing

Signs this may be true for you

  • You feel anxious making any significant decision without running it by multiple people.
  • You know what you want but don't feel like it's enough until someone agrees.
  • After asking everyone, you end up more confused than when you started.

Frequently asked

Why don't I trust my own judgment?

Usually because it was undermined — directly or indirectly — at an important point.

Is it bad to ask for others' opinions?

No — gathering perspectives is useful. The issue is when you can't feel settled without external validation.

How do I rebuild self-trust?

By making decisions without consulting others first, then noticing the outcome.
