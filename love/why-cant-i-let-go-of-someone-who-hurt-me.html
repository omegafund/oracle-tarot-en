Why Can't I Let Go of Someone Who Hurt Me?

You know they hurt you. You know you should move on. And yet — you can't stop thinking about them.

Letting go of someone who hurt you is one of the hardest emotional tasks there is. And it has almost nothing to do with willpower.

The short answer

The inability to let go after being hurt often isn't about the person — it's about something unfinished inside you that the relationship stirred up.

The question 'why can't I let go of someone who hurt me' is really asking: what is this attachment protecting me from having to feel?

What would you have to feel if you truly let this go?

Signs this resonates with you

  • You replay what happened over and over
  • You check their social media even when it hurts
  • You miss them and hate yourself for it
  • You dream about them or think about them daily
  • You keep hoping for an explanation or apology

Trauma bonding — where cycles of pain and relief create intense attachment — is one of the strongest reasons people can't let go, even knowing they should.

When there was no trauma bond and the relationship was simply comfortable, letting go tends to happen more naturally with time and distance.

Questions people ask

Why can't I let go even when I know they were bad for me?

Because the brain processes emotional bonds similarly to addiction — the withdrawal is real, not just weakness.

Does not letting go mean I still love them?

Not always. Sometimes it means the wound hasn't healed yet, not that the love is still alive.

How long does it take to let go?

There's no timeline. But actively processing the grief tends to move things faster than suppressing it.

What actually helps with letting go?

Grieving what you hoped it would be — not just what it was — tends to be the missing piece for most people.

Can tarot help me let go?

A reflection can help you identify what you're actually holding onto — which is often different from what you think it is.
