Why Do I Lose Myself in Relationships?

Created as a space for personal reflection — not therapy or diagnosis.

You're in the relationship, and you're happy — mostly.

But somewhere along the way, your own preferences and interests got quieter.

And you're not sure when that happened.

The short answer Losing yourself in relationships is usually about more than devotion. It's often about an identity that was somewhat fragile — or a belief that belonging to someone makes you more real.
Think of your last relationship: what did you stop doing, and did you notice when you stopped?

Signs this may be true for you

  • You find yourself adopting your partner's interests more than they adopt yours.
  • After a relationship ends, you feel unsure of who you are.
  • You feel guilty for wanting time to yourself.

Frequently asked

Why do I lose myself when I fall in love?

Love activates a merging impulse that overrides the separate-self boundary. This is amplified when the separate self felt shaky: if you weren't fully sure of who you were alone, belonging to someone can feel like the answer.

Is it possible to love deeply and still keep yourself?

Yes — this is what makes love sustainable. Maintaining your own interests and perspective doesn't threaten love; it sustains it.

How do I stay myself while being in a relationship?

It starts before you're in one: knowing what matters to you and what you won't give up.
