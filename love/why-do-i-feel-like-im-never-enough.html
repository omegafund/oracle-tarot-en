The short answer The 'not enough' feeling is almost never an accurate assessment of who you are. It's a belief formed early — in response to conditions that had nothing to do with your actual worth — now used to interpret every relationship.

Frequently asked

Why do I always feel like I'm not enough?

Usually because 'enough' was defined externally in your formative years — by an environment that communicated you needed to be different to be acceptable. The problem isn't your worth; it's the measuring stick.

Does this ever go away?

It shifts, rather than disappears. As you become more conscious of the belief and where it came from, it loses some of its authority.

How do I start believing I'm enough?

Less through affirmations and more through noticing when the 'not enough' voice appears and asking what evidence it's actually using.
