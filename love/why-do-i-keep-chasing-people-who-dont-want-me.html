Why Do I Keep Chasing People Who Don't Want Me?

Created as a space for personal reflection — not therapy or diagnosis.

You know they're not fully available. You feel it.

And yet something in you reaches anyway.

This reflection looks at what that reaching is really about.

The short answer The pull toward unavailable people is almost never about the specific person. It's about a deeper familiarity — trying to earn something that isn't freely given, which can feel more like love than love that actually comes easily.
What would it feel like if someone were consistently available — would that feel exciting or somehow flat?

Frequently asked

Why am I attracted to people who aren't interested in me?

Often because the pattern of pursuing what isn't freely given is familiar — it may recreate an early dynamic where love felt earned. The nervous system interprets the familiar as safe, even when the familiar is painful.

Is chasing unavailable people a trauma response?

It can be. When early caregiving was inconsistent, children learn to work for attention and interpret withholding as normal. In adult relationships, that learned response can make unavailability feel like love.

How do I break the pattern?

Notice the moment the chase begins — usually right after someone creates distance. That's the moment to pause rather than pursue.
