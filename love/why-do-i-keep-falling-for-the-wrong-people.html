The short answer Repeated attraction to "wrong" people often isn't random — it tends to follow a familiar emotional shape, one that mirrors an early relationship dynamic, even when it's painful. Familiar can feel like chemistry, even when it isn't good for you.

Frequently asked

Why am I attracted to people who are bad for me?

Attraction is often shaped by what's familiar, not necessarily what's healthy. If early relationships modeled unpredictability or emotional unavailability, that dynamic can register as chemistry later on.

Can this pattern actually be changed?

Yes, though it usually requires consciously noticing the pull toward familiar dynamics and practicing staying present with people who don't provide the same intensity, until that becomes comfortable too.

Does falling for the wrong people mean something is wrong with me?

No — it reflects a learned pattern, not a flaw. Patterns that were adaptive once can simply be outdated now, and they're changeable with awareness.
