Why Do I Keep Getting Ghosted?

Created as a space for personal reflection — not therapy or diagnosis.

They were there — and then they weren't.

No explanation, no closure. Just silence where there used to be a person.

If it's happened more than once, part of you wonders if there's something about you that makes people disappear.

The short answer Ghosting is almost always more about the person leaving than the person left. But a recurring pattern is worth examining — not to find fault with yourself, but to see whether something in how you connect or who you connect with is worth understanding differently.
Be honest: before the ghosting, was there a moment when you sensed something was off? What did you do with that?

  • You invest quickly and are then blindsided when they disappear.

Frequently asked

Why do people ghost instead of just saying something?

Ghosting is almost always conflict avoidance — the person disappearing doesn't know how to end something directly, or doesn't feel enough obligation to do it. It's rarely about you being unworthy of a response. It's about them not having the capacity for directness.

Does being ghosted repeatedly mean something about me?

A pattern is worth examining — but the examination should be curious, not self-punishing. It might point to selecting people with avoidant styles, or connecting quickly before you have real information about someone. Neither of those is a flaw; they're patterns that can be seen and shifted.

How do I stop being affected by ghosting?

The goal isn't indifference — it's investing in proportion to what's actually been established. Ghosting hurts more when a lot was projected onto an early connection. As you get clearer on what you actually know about someone versus what you've imagined, the disappearance of a near-stranger lands differently.
