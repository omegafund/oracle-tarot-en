Why Do I Feel Anxious When Things Are Going Well?

Created as a space for personal reflection — not therapy or diagnosis.

Everything is fine. Better than fine, actually.

And somehow that's exactly what's making you uneasy.

You keep waiting for the other shoe to drop, even though no one said it would.

The short answer When distress has been familiar for long enough, calm can register as danger rather than safety. Your nervous system may be scanning good moments for the catch, because in the past, good moments were often followed by one.
Ask yourself: what would happen if you let yourself believe this good thing is allowed to stay?

Frequently asked

Is it normal to feel anxious during good times?

Yes, especially if calm hasn't historically been safe or lasting. The anxiety isn't a sign anything is wrong now — it's often a delayed alarm reacting to an old pattern.

How do I stop waiting for something bad to happen?

Rather than trying to force the fear away, it helps to name what it's protecting against, and then gently test — in small ways — whether good things are allowed to continue without punishment.

Does this anxiety mean my intuition is warning me?

Not necessarily. Real intuition tends to be specific and quiet, while anticipatory anxiety is usually vague and constant. The difference often becomes clearer with a bit of distance from the feeling.
