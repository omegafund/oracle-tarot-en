Why Does Love Never Last for Me?

Created as a space for personal reflection — not therapy or diagnosis.

It starts with hope. It always does.

And somewhere — it ends. Again.

You start to wonder if it's you. If something about you makes love temporary.

The short answer When love keeps ending, the question worth asking isn't 'what's wrong with me' but 'what pattern keeps repeating' — because what ends relationships is usually more about recurring dynamics than fundamental unworthiness.
Think about the last two or three endings: what do they have in common that you haven't fully looked at?

Frequently asked

Why do my relationships keep ending?

Recurring endings usually follow a recurring pattern — either in who you choose, how you behave when intimacy deepens, or what you do when conflict arrives. The pattern isn't evidence that you can't sustain love; it's information about where the work is.

Is it possible some people just aren't meant for long-term love?

No — but people do have patterns that make certain relationship structures harder. An avoidant person will struggle in a relationship that requires constant closeness. An anxious person will struggle with someone emotionally unavailable. The question isn't compatibility with 'love' in general but with specific ways of relating.

How do I break a pattern of relationships ending?

Start by mapping the pattern rather than blaming it. What's consistent across the endings? What's your role in each? Not to assign fault — but to find where your choices or responses have been the same, because that's where change is actually available.
