Why Am I Scared of Being Truly Loved?

You want love. Real, deep, unconditional love. And somehow — when it gets close, you pull away.

Fear of being truly loved is more common than it sounds. Because real love requires being truly seen — and that's terrifying if being seen ever meant being rejected.

The short answer

Fear of being truly loved usually comes from a deep belief that if someone saw all of you — not just the acceptable parts — they would leave.

Fear of being truly loved often coexists with a deep longing for it — the two feelings living side by side, creating a push-pull that's exhausting to live inside.

When someone loves you consistently and openly, does it feel safe — or does something in you start looking for the catch?

Signs this resonates with you

  • You self-sabotage when relationships get serious
  • You find reasons to pull away when someone gets too close
  • Consistent love feels suspicious or suffocating
  • You're more comfortable loving than being loved
  • You push people away right when things feel good

This fear is most intense in people who experienced love as conditional — where approval was earned and withdrawal was used as punishment.

People who experienced consistent, unconditional love early on tend to receive love more easily — not because they're luckier, but because love doesn't feel like a trap.

Questions people ask

Is it normal to be scared of love?

Very. Especially for people whose early experiences taught them that closeness eventually leads to pain.

Why do I push away people who love me?

Often because part of you believes it's safer to leave before they do — or before they see who you really are.

Can I learn to let love in?

Yes. It's one of the most meaningful forms of healing — and it usually happens slowly, in small moments of allowing.

What does being truly loved actually require?

Being seen. Which means letting someone know the parts of you that feel least lovable — and experiencing that they stay.
