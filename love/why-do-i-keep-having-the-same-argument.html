Why Do I Keep Having the Same Argument?

Created as a space for personal reflection — not therapy or diagnosis.

It starts over something small. Or the same big thing.

And then it becomes something else — familiar, circular, exhausting.

You've had this argument before.

The short answer Recurring arguments are almost never really about the surface topic. They're about unmet needs or a dynamic that neither person has fully named.
Underneath the argument, what is it you're actually trying to say — and has your partner ever fully heard that?

Signs this may be true for you

  • The argument starts over different triggers but follows the same arc.
  • You each have established positions that don't really shift.
  • One or both of you shuts down at a certain point.

Frequently asked

Why do couples have the same argument over and over?

Because the argument is addressing a symptom, not the underlying issue. It keeps returning because the underlying need hasn't been met.

Is it a bad sign if we keep arguing about the same thing?

Not necessarily — it depends on whether the argument shows any movement.

How do we break a recurring argument cycle?

By stepping outside the content and talking about the argument itself: 'We keep having this fight. What do you think it's really about?'
