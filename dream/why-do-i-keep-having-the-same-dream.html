Frequently asked

Why do I keep having the same recurring dream?

Recurring dreams typically reflect something unresolved that the mind keeps revisiting — an emotion, a fear, or a situation that hasn’t been fully processed or addressed. The repetition often continues until the underlying material is acknowledged in waking life. Paying attention to the feeling the dream produces, rather than only its imagery, usually points toward what it’s working on.

Do recurring dreams have a meaning?

They tend to carry personal significance, though not a fixed universal meaning. The same dream means different things for different people, shaped by what the imagery and feeling represent for you. Rather than looking up symbols in a dictionary, it’s usually more revealing to ask what the dream’s emotion mirrors in your current life — that’s where the meaning lives.

How do I stop a recurring dream?

Recurring dreams often ease once the waking situation or feeling they reflect gets acknowledged and addressed. Rather than trying to suppress the dream, it tends to help to sit with the emotion it leaves and ask what in your life is echoing it. As the underlying tension is processed, the loop usually loosens on its own.
