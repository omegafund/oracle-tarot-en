Why Do I Dream About My Ex?

Created as a space for personal reflection — not therapy or diagnosis.

They showed up in your dream last night.

You woke up unsettled — maybe with feelings you thought you'd moved past.

And now you're wondering what it means that they're still in your sleep.

The short answer Dreams about exes are extremely common and rarely literal. They're usually less about the person and more about what they represented — a feeling, a time in your life, an unresolved question that your waking mind hasn't finished with.
In the dream, how did they make you feel — and is that feeling familiar from somewhere other than them?

Frequently asked

Why do I keep dreaming about someone I'm over?

'Over' is rarely total. You can be genuinely done with a relationship and still have your mind revisit it — especially during stress or transitions, when the unconscious tends to process earlier experiences. Dreams aren't votes on whether you're over something; they're the mind's way of sorting unfinished material.

Does dreaming about an ex mean I still love them?

Not necessarily. Dreams about exes often reflect processing — of the ending, of who you were in that relationship, of what you wanted and didn't get. Love in a dream doesn't translate to love in a conscious, actionable sense. It may be nostalgia, or grief, or simply unfinished processing.

How do I stop having dreams about an ex?

You largely can't control dream content directly — but what you do with the waking feelings matters. If the dreams are highlighting something unprocessed — grief, anger, unanswered questions — finding a way to address those while awake often reduces their presence in sleep.
