Why Do I Always Dream About Being Chased?

Created as a space for personal reflection — not therapy or diagnosis.

In the dream, something is behind you.

You run but can't move fast enough, or the distance never grows.

You wake up with your heart already racing.

The short answer Being chased in dreams is almost universally understood as a symbol of avoidance — something in your life that you're running from rather than facing. The threat in the dream is usually internal: a feeling, a situation, a truth.

Frequently asked

What does a chasing dream usually mean?

Chase dreams almost universally point to avoidance — something in waking life you're running from rather than engaging with. The threat in the dream is usually symbolic: a feeling, a decision, a truth that's been deferred. The dream is your mind's dramatic way of saying 'you can't keep outrunning this.'

Why can't I move fast enough in chase dreams?

The frustrating running-in-slow-motion element reflects the experience of avoidance itself — the more you try to escape something through avoidance rather than addressing it, the heavier and more effortful the avoiding becomes. The slowness is the dream's commentary on the strategy, not on your physical capabilities.

How do I stop having chase dreams?

Rarely by changing the dreams directly. More often by looking at what the chasing represents. If there's a decision you've been avoiding, a conversation you've been postponing, a feeling you've been running from — addressing that in waking life often changes the dream landscape.
