Frequently asked

How do I understand what my subconscious is telling me?

The subconscious tends to communicate through feeling and recurrence rather than clear statements — a theme that keeps surfacing, an emotion that returns, a quiet sense you keep dismissing. Understanding it is less about decoding symbols from a dictionary and more about noticing what keeps showing up and giving it honest attention. Often it’s surfacing something you already half-know.

Can my dreams reveal what I really want?

Dreams can surface desires and feelings the waking mind suppresses or overrides, so they sometimes reveal what you genuinely want beneath what you think you should want. They’re not infallible, but they bypass some of the self-editing of waking thought. Treating them as one honest input — reflection rather than instruction — tends to be the most useful approach.

Is my intuition the same as my subconscious?

They overlap. Intuition is often the subconscious surfacing a conclusion it reached from patterns and information you absorbed without conscious notice. It can feel like a message from somewhere else, but it’s usually your own accumulated knowing arriving faster than analysis. That doesn’t make it always right — but it’s worth listening to rather than dismissing.
