Why Do I Feel Like a Burden to Others?

Created as a space for personal reflection — not therapy or diagnosis.

You hesitate before asking for help. You apologize for needing things.

You minimize your problems before sharing them.

Somewhere, you've learned that your needs are too much.

The short answer The belief that you're a burden is almost always a learned belief, not an accurate one. It forms in environments where your needs were treated as inconvenient.
Think of a time when someone genuinely wanted to help you — what stopped you from fully receiving it?
🔄 Updated July 2026

What this reflection can reveal

  • where the belief was formed
  • how it manifests in relationships — who you reach out to and how
  • what it would mean about you if you let someone help

Signs this may be true for you

  • You don't ask for help even when you're struggling.
  • You apologize extensively when you do reach out.
  • You feel relief when you manage things alone, and guilt when you need others.

Frequently asked

Why do I feel like a burden?

Usually because you were treated as one at some point — or the people you depended on communicated that your needs were too much.

Is it ever true that I'm asking for too much?

Sometimes, yes — but the people who struggle most with feeling like a burden are usually asking for far less than the people who care about them would give.

How do I stop feeling like a burden?

By testing the belief rather than assuming it's true. Ask for something small. Notice what actually happens.
