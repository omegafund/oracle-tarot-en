Why Do I Feel Exhausted All the Time?

Created as a space for personal reflection — not therapy or diagnosis.

You sleep. You still wake up tired.

Or you rest, but nothing actually restores.

The exhaustion isn't in your body the way it should be.

The short answer Persistent exhaustion that doesn't respond to sleep or rest is often emotional or psychological in origin. It can come from sustained emotional labor, suppressed feelings, low-level chronic stress, or the effort of managing a life that doesn't fit who you actually are.

Frequently asked

Why am I always tired even when I sleep enough?

Emotional and psychological exhaustion doesn't respond to sleep the way physical fatigue does. If you're depleted by sustained anxiety, emotional suppression, chronic low-level stress, or the ongoing effort of performing okay when you're not, sleep restores the body but not the reserves that have been draining.

What is emotional exhaustion?

Emotional exhaustion is depletion that comes from sustained emotional labor — managing your feelings, managing others' feelings, being in situations that require constant vigilance or performance. It doesn't feel like tired so much as empty, flat, or unable to generate the energy that used to come naturally.

How do I recover from deep exhaustion?

Rest is necessary but not sufficient. You also need to reduce or remove what's draining you, which often requires honesty about what's actually demanding your energy. Sometimes that means boundaries. Sometimes it means grieving something you've been refusing to. Sometimes it means letting something be what it is rather than managing it.
