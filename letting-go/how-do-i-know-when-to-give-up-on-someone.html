How Do I Know When to Give Up on Someone?

Created as a space for personal reflection — not therapy or diagnosis.

You've tried. More than once, more than most people would.

And you're still not sure if this is perseverance or avoidance of an ending.

There's a difference between giving up and letting go.

The short answer Knowing when to give up isn't about reaching a point of no longer caring. It's about recognizing the difference between a relationship going through something difficult and a pattern that has shown you, repeatedly, what it actually is.
If the pattern in this relationship continued exactly as it has for the next five years, would you be glad you stayed?

Frequently asked

How do I know when a relationship is truly over?

When the pattern has shown you what it is — not once but repeatedly — and the gap between what's promised and what shows up hasn't closed despite genuine effort.

Is there ever a right time to give up?

There's rarely a perfect time. What tends to shift the decision isn't more information but more honesty about what the relationship actually is.

What's the difference between giving up and letting go?

Giving up implies failure; letting go implies completion after genuine effort and clear-eyed understanding.
