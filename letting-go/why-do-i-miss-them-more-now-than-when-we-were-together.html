Why Do I Miss Them More Now Than When We Were Together?

Created as a space for personal reflection — not therapy or diagnosis.

When you were together, there were problems. Real ones.

Now that it's over, those problems are somehow less visible.

You miss something that, in its actual form, often made you unhappy.

The short answer Missing someone more after it ends is partly the mind's natural selective memory — it edits toward the positive when you're no longer in it.
If you list five things that made the relationship genuinely difficult — can you hold those alongside the missing?

Signs this may be true for you

  • The relationship felt more complicated when you were in it than in retrospect.
  • You find yourself remembering specific good moments with unusual clarity.
  • You've forgotten or minimized some of the harder parts.

Frequently asked

Why do I miss someone who made me unhappy?

Because memory isn't objective — it tends to surface positive moments more readily than painful ones after an ending.

Is my mind romanticizing the relationship?

Probably, at least partly. Deliberately recalling specific difficult moments can help rebalance the picture.

Should I reach out if I miss them this much?

Not based on the missing alone. Ask: what would going back actually look like, given the reality of the relationship?
