Why Can't I Stop Thinking About What Could Have Been?

Created as a space for personal reflection — not therapy or diagnosis.

It wasn't just the relationship you lost. It was a version of your future.

The plans, the person you were becoming with them.

What could have been can feel as real as what actually was.

The short answer Grieving what could have been is legitimate — one that's often harder to talk about because it's not about what existed but what you imagined. That imagined future was real to you.

Frequently asked

Is it normal to grieve a future that never happened?

Completely. The brain doesn't cleanly distinguish between what was real and what was vividly imagined — loss of an expected future registers as loss, full stop.

How do I stop obsessing over what could have been?

By grieving it properly rather than trying to stop the thoughts. When you let yourself feel the sadness of the unlived future, it gradually loses its hold.

Does thinking about what could have been mean I should try again?

Not necessarily — the mind can return to a possible future without that being instruction to pursue it.
