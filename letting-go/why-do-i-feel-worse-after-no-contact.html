Why Do I Feel Worse After No Contact?

Created as a space for personal reflection — not therapy or diagnosis.

You did the right thing. You cut the contact.

Instead, you feel worse — more anxious, more missing them, more lost.

This is not what you planned.

The short answer Feeling worse at the start of no contact is nearly universal and doesn't mean the decision was wrong. The nervous system is experiencing the withdrawal of something it had become organized around.

Frequently asked

Why does no contact feel so painful?

Because the brain had organized itself around this person. When contact stops, that orientation system has nowhere to go.

Does feeling worse mean no contact isn't working?

No — feeling worse initially is very common and doesn't indicate the decision was wrong.

How long does the no-contact pain typically last?

Most people notice a meaningful shift between two and six weeks.
