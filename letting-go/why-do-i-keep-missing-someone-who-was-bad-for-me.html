Why Do I Keep Missing Someone Who Was Bad for Me?

Created as a space for personal reflection — not therapy or diagnosis.

You remember how it actually was. You remember why it ended.

And still, in the quiet moments, you miss them — which feels like a betrayal of everything you know.

This reflection looks at why the longing outlasts the logic.

The short answer Missing someone who was bad for you rarely means you should go back — it usually means your mind is holding onto the good moments and the hope attached to them more vividly than the harm. Longing isn’t a verdict on the relationship; it’s grief that hasn’t finished, often mixed with the unfinished hope that they could have been who you needed.
Ask yourself: are you missing them as they actually were, or the version of them you kept hoping for?

Frequently asked

Why do I miss someone who treated me badly?

The mind doesn’t archive a relationship as a balanced summary — it preserves the peaks, the hope, and the moments of connection more strongly than the steady harm. So when you miss them, you’re often missing those preserved highs and the future you imagined, not the daily reality. Recognizing this gap helps the longing make sense without it pulling you back.

Does missing my ex mean I should get back together?

Almost never on its own. Missing someone is an emotional state, not a decision-making signal. The feelings and the reasons it ended can coexist — you can genuinely miss them and still know returning would repeat the harm. Longing tells you that you’re grieving; it doesn’t tell you that going back is wise.

How long does it take to stop missing someone?

There’s no fixed timeline, and it rarely fades in a straight line — it comes in waves that gradually spread further apart. What shortens it isn’t forcing the feeling away but letting yourself actually grieve the loss and the hope, rather than looping on it. The missing softens as the unfinished feeling gets finished internally.
